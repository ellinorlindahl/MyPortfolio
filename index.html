How can I help you?

My goal in any design process is to create intentional, intuitive and invisible design by creating and maintaining a solid understanding and connection with your users directly.

I'm still soaking up everything I can in the different UX fields, but I can already feel my heart beat a little extra hard for information architecture (I love the pursuit of the ultimate logic, clarity and structure) and UX writing - where commucation and language meets design.

Information architecture: Organizing information in a structured and intuitive way.
UX research: Connecting with your users and creating a deep understanding of their needs.
UX writing: Designing a clear and helpful experience by using words.
Methods: Design Thinking / Indepth interviewing / Thematic analysis / Workshops / User Journeys / Impact mapping / Cognitive walkthough / and more.
Tools: Figma / Miro / Visual Studio Code / Balsamiq / and open to learning many more!

Some UX projects I've learned alot from

PriceRunner | Internship | 2021

An 9 week in-depth evaluation of Pricerunner's digital workspace / internal operations system. Presenting research findings, explaining and justifying design proposals to product owners and management team.

      Methods used:
  • Heuristic analysis
  • Benchmarking
  • User surveys
  • User interviews
  • Cognitive walkthrough
  • Open card sorting
  • An iterative ideation process

Arbetsförmedlingen | 2020

A project focused on user testing. As a part of 8 week course 'UX-process 2: User centered design', we did a group project for Arbetsförmedlingen.
The mission was to test and evaluate a specific existing user flow on Arbetsförmedlingens webpage.

      Methods used:
  • Design Thinking
  • In-depth user interviews
  • User testing
  • HMW workshop + Lightning Decision Jam
  • Impact Effort Mapping

FRISQ | 2020

A research focused project as part of 8 week course 'UX-process 1: Analysis & Strategy'. FRISQ, a company providing digital healthcare solutions asked us to investgate how we might increase patient engagement outside of the digital sphere.

      Methods used:
  • Design Thinking
  • In-depth interviews + Thematic analysis
  • Quantitative surveys
  • Personas
  • Effect mapping + Impact mapping
  • User journeys
  • Wireframes
