FRISQ

A research focused case study

Client:

FRISQ, a company developing digital tools for a person-centric healthcare. Their digital solutions strive to improve patient experience and health outcomes.

Case:

Patients who actively participate in their own care both has better health and redduces the costs of healthcare.
"How can we increase patient engagement outside of the digital sphere?".
Our goal was to develop ideas, both within and beyond the digital sphere, that would increase the patients engagement in planning and controlling their own care.

Process:

  • Defining & Recruiting target users
  • In-depth interviews + Thematic analysis
  • Quantitative surveys
  • Personas
  • User journeys
  • Develop 'How Might We'-statement
  • Value proposition canvas
  • Impact mapping
  • Ideation workshop
  • Final recommendations & Presentation
    • And a box about who was in the team and how long it took and who was supervisor! (part of an eight week course in 'UX-process: Analysis & Strategy' with Andreas Olsson).

    Defining patient engagement

    After researching and discussing the concept of patient engagement, we narrowed the definition down to these four key elements: understanding, empowerment, trust and security. We used these key words as baseline parameters throughout our research process, and used them when designing qualitative interview questions and quantitative questionnaires.

    In-depth interviews + Thematic analysis

    We conducted five in-depth interviews with people from our target user group. We then used our transcriptions to make a thematic analysis and found these four key insights:

  • Human connection is important. The patients wants to be seen and heard by their health care provider.
  • Clear information exchange is important. The patient needs to comprehend all care instructions, and want their care providers to have a solid understanding of their medical history.
  • Time is important. Patients felt like their care providers did not have enough time for them or read their journals, and they seldom met the same doctor consistently thoughout their care process.
  • Coordination is important. Patients felt the lacking coordination between different health care systems created problems when trying to find the appropriate care for them, getting in touch with the right person or when there was a need to share information between departments or organisations.

    • We also conducted five interviews with medical proffessionals: a nurse, a general practician, an intensive unit doctor, and two specialist doctors. These were our key take aways from those interviews:

  • Too much time is spent on admin and documentation, due to poorly designed systems.
  • Coordination and communication between health care units regarding a mutual patient is difficult.
  • There is no overview anywhere of a patients medical history, only pages and pages of journals.

    Impact map

    We visualized the research insights and the connection between our ideas and the needs of the patients with an impact map.

    Ending words and recommendations

    There is alot to be done to increase patient engagement, and our research suggests three key focus areas to FRISQ moving forward:

  • Providing an overview of a patients medical history
  • Aiding coordination and communication between health care units
  • Improve the line of communication between patient and their care provider

    • Rather than "creating yet another app" prompting patients to perform actions, we suggest FRISQ to work within the existing digital frameworks of the Swedish health care system.
